Causes of Marine Debris

Where Does Marine Debris Come From?

Marine debris has many different sources, but most of it originates from human activity. Improperly managed waste, careless habits, and unsustainable production all contribute to the growing problem of trash in the ocean.

Main Causes

  • Improper Waste Disposal: Trash thrown on the ground, streets, or beaches can easily be carried by wind or rain into waterways that flow into the sea.
  • Single-Use Plastics: Everyday items like bags, bottles, straws, and food wrappers are used once and often discarded without recycling.
  • Fishing Industry: Lost or abandoned nets, lines, and traps (known as "ghost gear") account for a large share of ocean debris.
  • Shipping and Boating: Waste and cargo accidentally or illegally dumped into the sea from vessels.
  • Natural Disasters: Floods, storms, and hurricanes can wash huge amounts of litter and debris from land into the ocean.

A Human-Made Problem

Marine debris is ultimately caused by human choices. The convenience of plastics, poor waste management systems, and lack of awareness all combine to push more and more trash into the ocean. Because of this, it is a problem that people also have the power to solve.
