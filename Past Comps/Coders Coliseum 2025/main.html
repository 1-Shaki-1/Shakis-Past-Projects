Marine debris travels far!

Plastic waste thrown into the ocean can float thousands of miles and even end up on completely different continents. Some items from Asia have washed up on U.S. beaches years later.

?

Animals mistake plastic for food.

Sea turtles often think floating plastic bags are jellyfish, one of their favorite meals. Sadly, eating them can block their stomachs.

?

80% of ocean debris comes from land.

Most marine debris doesn’t come from ships — it comes from trash on land that gets blown or washed into rivers, then flows to the sea.
